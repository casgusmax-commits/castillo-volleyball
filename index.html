About Gustavo

VOLLEYBALL IS A THINKING GAME.

Most development programs in Canada focus on physical output — vertical jump, strength, conditioning. Those things matter. But they don't explain why some athletes read the game two steps ahead while others react a step too late.

After 30+ years working with national teams, professional clubs, and elite structures across four continents, I've built my methodology around one conviction: the athlete who reads the game best wins it. Technique without tactical intelligence is half the equation.

My approach integrates the I.E.R. model — Interpret, Elaborate, Resolve — applied in every session, every analysis, every feedback. Each match generates a statistical profile and a detailed video breakdown: what happened, why it happened, and what to do differently. The brain learns where it practices. So we train the complete game: with real-game rhythm, genuine uncertainty, and decision-making under time pressure.

The I.E.R. Model

INTERPRET · ELABORATE · RESOLVE

I
Interpret
Read the situation before committing to a response. Detect opponent cues, court positioning, and attack trajectories. Elite athletes don't react faster — they perceive better.
E
Elaborate
Generate options and select the most efficient one in milliseconds. Inhibit the automatic response. Patience under pressure is a trainable skill — not a personality trait.
R
Resolve
Execute with technical precision and mental clarity. Technique is the consequence, not the starting point. We train execution in context — not in isolation.
